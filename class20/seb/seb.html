WINDS_OF_KYOTO

by_Sebastian_Wilhelm

Winds of Kyoto is a narrative music concrete piece as an homage to a trip to Kyoto that didnâ€™t take place due to the outbreak of covid. As the name suggests the piece focuses on sounds from Japanese culture propagated by the wind. The audio composition was realized in sonic pi by procedurally composing instruments out of samples and additive synthesis and then into progressively more complex arrangements resulting in sections. The five sections tell a story about a lively monastery focusing on rhythmical martial sounds from a monk training paired with a natural and peaceful atmospheric backdrop. As the day approaches its end the composition takes you downstream into a hidden thicket where a secret sect is performing a mischievous ritual, with the aim of summoning a demon. The climax of the piece is the fight between the monks and the beast. After an intense fight, the initial peace has been restored. Or so it seems. The visual motif was procedurally built by aligning and connecting vertices sorted in a linear array. Resulting in an origami-like strip, which reinforces the Japanese theme. The strip grows over the duration of the piece giving an indication of time as well as twisting, turning, and expanding based on different frequency ranges of the sounds, assuming the role of a simple spectrogram. After some experimentation with a shader, it has been used to infuse the origami-like structure with internal mystical energy.
